🏰 Must See
Windsor Castle
The oldest and largest occupied castle in the world. Founded by William the Conqueror in 1066, home to 40 monarchs. St George's Chapel is a medieval marvel — Henry VIII, Charles I, and Queen Elizabeth II are buried here. Allow at least 2 hours. Visit St George's Chapel first as queues form quickly.
Free Time in Windsor Town
🏠
The Crooked House (Market Cross House)
Famously wonky building on the High Street, said to have a secret tunnel to the castle. Great photo stop.
🌳
The Long Walk — Cambridge Gate
Walk from the Crooked House to Cambridge Gate (5 min). Peer through the gates for views up the 2.5-mile Long Walk to the Copper Horse statue (George III on horseback). It was here that Queen Elizabeth II's funeral procession began its final approach.
🍺
The Two Brewers
Great pub right beside Cambridge Gate. Perfect spot for lunch with a view of the castle's private entrance.
🌿 For Devon
The Cavendish family (Dukes of Devonshire) appear throughout Windsor Castle's royal court history — keep an eye out in the State Apartments. Devonshire clotted cream teas are also widely available in Windsor town.
⚠ Don't miss the coach
Meet at the replica train outside Windsor & Eton Central station in the Windsor Royal Shopping Centre at 2:30 PM sharp. The coach waits for no one.

Greenwich Sights
⛵
Cutty Sark
The world's only surviving extreme clipper ship, 150 years old. Travelled the equivalent of 2.5 voyages to the moon and back.
Paid entry · daytime
🔭
Royal Observatory & Meridian Line
Founded 1675 to solve the longitude problem. The line of 0° longitude runs through here. Climb the hill for a spectacular panorama — Canary Wharf, Thames curve, St Paul's, London Eye, The Shard, all at once.
Free outside · Paid entry
🦶
Greenwich Foot Tunnel (7:30 PM)
Walk under the Thames via this Victorian engineering marvel (opened 1902). From the north bank, DLR from Island Gardens to Canary Wharf.
Canary Wharf
🌿
Crossrail Place Roof Garden
Hidden rooftop garden above Canary Wharf station sitting almost exactly on the Meridian line. Asian plants to the east, Americas to the west. Genuinely stunning and almost unknown to tourists.
🎨
Outdoor Art Collection
London's largest collection of outdoor public art — 100+ pieces transforming the financial district into an open-air gallery.
💡
The Outernet — 'The Now Building'
Exit Tottenham Court Road station via Charing Cross Road exit. Four storeys of wrap-around LED screens — a genuinely spectacular free light show.
🏛 History
Greenwich is the birthplace of Henry VIII, Mary I, and Elizabeth I. The Palace of Placentia once stood on the riverside — Henry VIII was born, married, and hunted here. The Old Royal Naval College (designed by Wren) later became the world's first naval college.

Victorian coal storage vaults transformed into a stunning retail and dining destination. Cobbled courtyards, great restaurants, independent shops. Visit Granary Square — 1,080 illuminated waterjets at night.

Regent's Canal & Gasholder Park
Stroll along the canal (completed 1820). Gasholder No 8 — a 19th century wrought-iron structure, dismantled and restored, now encasing a circular lawn. A beautiful and unusual space.
🏛 History: King's Cross
Believed to be the site of the legendary battle between Queen Boudicca and Roman invaders in AD 61. Boudicca's final resting place is said to be under Platform 9 at King's Cross! The area was known as Battle Bridge until a statue of George IV was erected in 1830 — demolished 1842, but the name King's Cross stuck.

🏛 History Highlights
Roman London (AD 43–410) — Londinium founded by Romans. Walls, amphitheatre (Guildhall), and Mithras Temple (Bloomberg Space) survive. Tower Hill area
Viking London (9th–11th c) — Danes sacked London multiple times. Alfred the Great recaptured it 886 AD. The street name "Aldwych" means "old settlement" — where Danes lived. Strand / Aldwych area
Tower of London (1066–present) — William the Conqueror built it to intimidate Londoners. Elizabeth I imprisoned here, Anne Boleyn beheaded, Guy Fawkes tortured. Crown Jewels on display. Walking distance from hotel
Great Fire of London (1666) — Destroyed 13,200 houses and 87 churches over 4 days. Sir Christopher Wren redesigned the City — St Paul's, The Monument, 50+ churches all his work. City of London
WWII London (1940–45) — The Blitz killed 30,000 Londoners. Churchill's Cabinet War Rooms are preserved beneath Whitehall. St Paul's famously survived the Blitz. Westminster / City
Windsor Castle (1070–present) — Built by William the Conqueror, home to 40 monarchs over 950 years. Largest occupied castle in the world. Queen Elizabeth II is buried in St George's Chapel. Windsor · Fri excursion
💡 Practical Tips
Oyster Card — Always tap in AND out at every station. Forgetting to tap out = maximum fare charged. Top up at any Tube station. Provided at hotel check-in
Payment — Contactless credit/debit cards work everywhere. London is essentially cashless. Apple Pay widely accepted.
Tipping — 10–12.5% at restaurants (check if service already included). Not expected in pubs. Taxi: round up to nearest pound.
Weather in June — Average 19°C (66°F). Can rain anytime. Bring a light layer and a compact umbrella. Evenings can be cool.
Pub hours — Most pubs open until 11pm Mon–Sat, 10:30pm Sunday. Sunday closing is earlier across most of London.
Cab apps — Uber works in London. Black cabs (official London taxis) can be hailed on the street — all licensed and metered.
Emergency — 999 (UK emergency number, equivalent to 911). Non-emergency police: 101.
